What we believe

The Bad News

There is a great God that created everyone so that we might know and love him. However, each of us has been wicked (Rom 3.23). We have rebelled against this great and loving God. Though he created us, we’ve acted like we are greater than him and have served ourselves instead.

We are not good people who make mistakes now and then. We have been selfishly working against God and we will face the consequences from this just God (Romans 1.18).

There is nothing we can do to remove this guilt from ourselves. We will inevitably face God’s judgment.

The Good News

Yet, even given all of this, God has made a way so that we can be rescued. His son Jesus came to earth as a human to die for our guilt. Jesus saved us in a way like no one else. He took our penalty, bore the cost of our guilt.

Because of this amazing act of grace, if we humbly turn to him in sorrow for our guilt (James 4.6-10), he will show us mercy and forgive us for our wickedness!

What a wonderful God, that he would love us even while we hated him (Romans 5.6-11)! There is no one else we can turn to who will love us so faithfully and graciously.

Thanks to God, he raised Jesus from death back to life, proving that he is God’s son and that all he did and taught was what we should follow and obey – and our resurrection.

Your Response?

In the first speech given about Jesus after he had done all of this for us, one of Jesus’ followers told the crowd that God was giving his Spirit so that everyone who calls on the name of Jesus will be rescued. When the crowds realized that they had been involved with murdering Jesus, they didn’t know what to do. Peter told them to turn away from their wickedness and be immersed in water (baptized) in order to be forgiven of their guilt and in order to receive the gift of God’s Spirit (Acts 2).

God gave us his Spirit to purify us. He saved us so that, as his people, we would be enthusiastic about doing good (Titus 2). Christians are not to be stagnate, but are to continually pursue Jesus Christ, to share in his suffering and in his resurrection (Philippians 3).

Of course, not everyone who hears this message believes it. If you doubt all this, don’t turn away without first looking into the claim that Jesus was resurrected. This has been the foundational claim of Christianity since the very beginning and cannot reasonably be explained away.
